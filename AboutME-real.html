How would you honestly describe the Wizard of Oz after seeing behind the curtain, and not just seeing behind it but crawling through the mechanisms that work together to create the illusion of an all-powerful wizard? Fixing broken parts and improving the marriage. The only honest answer I see is that the wizard is a guy pupeting on a mask to seem powerful. I must comprehend all of human knowledge before I die, and I don't think I have long left.

But I have thought long and hard about exactly what I am and how I need to improve. My brain generates thoughts and gathers knowledge, so I need to change it to do so more efficiently. Once you see the chains of neurons and all the systems that make "you" up, it is hard to think you are anything other than a fleshy robot designed by evolution to ensure the survival of your genes. I don't care for this purpose, but that doesn't matter. I now know what I am, and I won't go back to lying to myself. To be metaphorical about it, I have traded the comfort of a soul for a better understanding of reality and myself. Many people think this leads to the destruction of morals, but it doesn't. If a robot kills someone, it is wrong regardless of whether its programming made it, because its programming is it and maintains and changes itself. So if a rogue robot kills someone, we do still direct the blame to the robot. We are deterministic brains, so the brain doesn't make itself do anything; it just does it.
