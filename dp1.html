The Responsibility for Learning

These two artefacts represent the responsibility for learning because they were self-guided. On the right is my astronomy final project. This was a self-guided assignment in which I picked a topic and conducted research on it. This is taking responsibility for my learning by putting it in my hands. Above is an assignment on Hubble's law. In this assignment, I had an idea to obtain a better estimate of Hubble's constant from the data, and I took responsibility for my learning by developing and testing this method.
      The responsibility for learning is the ability to learn on your own, an invaluable skill required to continue growing outside of school. Being able and willing to think about a topic on your own and understand that topic on your own is the backbone of personal growth. In my opinion, you don't know the quadratic formula until you can derive it by hand from the basic algebraic rules. This is because a formula describes something, and deriving that formula makes your brain think through the connections that the original discoverer (Euler) had to make.
